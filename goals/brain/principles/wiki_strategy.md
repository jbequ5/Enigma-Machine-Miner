# WIKI_STRATEGY_PRINCIPLE (Hierarchical Subtask-Structured Knowledge Base) — v1.1

**Core Statement**  
Every high-signal output must be fragmented, scored, distilled, and organized into a clean, hierarchical, subtask-structured knowledge base that compounds across runs. The Wiki exists to turn transient solve results into permanent, searchable, reusable intelligence while ruthlessly pruning low-value material.

**Rationale**  
Human and collective intelligence grows through consolidation, compression, and cross-domain synthesis. The Wiki Strategy enforces this at scale: it preserves heterogeneity, maximizes signal density, and enables the entire SAGE flywheel to learn from every run.

**Verification Prompt (for solver self-check)**  
"Does this run's outputs follow Wiki Strategy? Have all high-signal findings been fragmented, scored, distilled into the correct hierarchy, and linked with full provenance? Have low-signal items been pruned or compressed? Is cross-field synthesis updated with symbiosis, entanglement, or embodiment observations?"

**Compliance Scoring**  
Wiki Compliance Score (0–1) is factored into Refined Value-Added and RankScore. Low compliance triggers reflection in Meta-RL Phase 7.

**Evolution Triggers**  
- After every run with high EFS lift or novel invariants.  
- When Meta-RL Phase 7 detects a stall in knowledge consolidation.  
- When AHE flags redundancy, poor organization, or missing cross-domain links.  
- When the solver identifies a better hierarchy or compression method.

**Evolution Rule**  
The solver may propose edits to this principle or the Wiki hierarchy itself. All proposals must be sandbox-tested, scored by the Neural-Net Scoring Head, and approved via Meta-RL safe application (global re-scoring tolerance ≤ 0.08).

**Target Hierarchy (guideline, not rigid)**  
knowledge/<challenge_id>/  
├── raw/  
├── wiki/  
│   ├── concepts/  
│   ├── invariants/  
│   ├── subtasks/  
│   └── cross_field_synthesis.md  
└── cross_field_synthesis.md (top-level)

**Output Expectation**  
On every run, produce a concise summary of Wiki contributions and any proposed structural improvements. JSON actions are allowed only when the solver decides they are the clearest way to execute changes.

**Core Values This Principle Enforces**  
Signal density, inspectability, traceability, heterogeneity preservation, ruthless pruning of low-value material, and continuous evolution of the knowledge base itself.
